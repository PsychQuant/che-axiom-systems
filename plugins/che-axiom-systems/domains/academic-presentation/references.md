# References — academic-presentation domain

External sources that ground and corroborate the axioms. The domain was first
distilled from the IMPS 2026 slide build and rehearsal feedback (2026-07-18);
these references show the same principles in published presentation guidance.

## R1. Chambers (2014), APA gradPSYCH

> Chambers, R. (2014). *Presenting your research effectively.* gradPSYCH
> Magazine, p. 28. American Psychological Association.
> https://www.apa.org/gradpsych/2014/09/presenting-research (archived)

APA's own graduate-student guidance on giving a research talk. It corroborates
several axioms and adds a few design specifics.

### Additional design specifics (not yet axiomatized)

Corroborating but finer-grained than the current axiom set; candidates for
future extension if they recur:

- **Colour palette** — ~3 complementary colours; be aware of colour affect
  (blue calming, red aggressive).
- **Animation** — usually not worth it; distracting; use only to add emphasis
  where value is clear.
- **Font / readability** — high contrast, easy-to-read font; font "personality"
  matters (Comic Sans inappropriate for a conference).

### One noted divergence — bullet points

Chambers advises **against** bullet points: *"Sometimes you may feel a need to
use bullet points. Do not. Typesetting can replace bullet points... consider
bolding and increasing the font size of parent lines and indenting child
lines."*

This diverges from the working practice adopted in the IMPS build, where the
user asked for **formal bullet lists** ("正式列點") for the format-description
slides. Both are defensible: Chambers optimises for a billboard-clean look;
formal bullets optimise for a scannable, parallel structure the speaker can read
under A2/T2 (verbatim-slide crutch). Recorded as a genuine trade-off, not
silently resolved — the choice depends on whether the slide is a
few-words billboard or a read-along script surface.
